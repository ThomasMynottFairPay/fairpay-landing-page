# FairPay Church Giving Campaign - Implementation Guide

## Campaign Strategy Overview

### Campaign Name
**"Set it up now (not later)"**

### Core Problem
Churches lose potential recurring givers because:
1. Members have high intention in-service but low follow-through later
2. Traditional bank setup (BSB/account details) creates friction, especially for 18-40s
3. Admin teams spend excessive time chasing failed payments and reconciling

### Solution Positioning
FairPay provides the PayTo **workflow layer** (not just rails):
- Instant in-service setup via QR/link
- Bank-app approval (trusted, no forms)
- Automated recurring collection with retries
- Dashboard visibility and reconciliation

## üí¨ Objection Handling

### Objection: "Our congregation prefers cash/check"
**Response:** 
"We get it ‚Äî and FairPay doesn't replace that. It's an *option* for the 18-40s who are already mobile-first. Churches typically see 30-40% of younger members adopt it immediately because it matches how they already bank."

### Objection: "Is this secure? What about their bank details?"
**Response:**
"Great question! Members *never* share bank details with the church. They approve the mandate inside their own banking app (the same app they use every day). It's bank-level security, and they can cancel anytime directly in their app."

### Objection: "What if someone wants to stop giving?"
**Response:**
"They stay in complete control. They can pause, change the amount, or cancel anytime directly in their banking app. No need to contact the church office or fill out forms."

### Objection: "How much does this cost?"
**Response:**
"FairPay has no setup fees and typically costs less than traditional direct debit (lower transaction fees + no dishonour fees). Plus, you'll save 8+ hours/month in admin time. We can walk you through the exact pricing in a quick 15-minute call."

### Objection: "We already use [existing giving platform]"
**Response:**
"FairPay integrates with most platforms, and many churches use both ‚Äî cards for one-time gifts, PayTo for recurring. The difference is the *in-service setup workflow*. That's where churches see the 40% uplift in recurring givers."
